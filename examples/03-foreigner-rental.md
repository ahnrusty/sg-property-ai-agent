# Example 3: Foreigner Renting in Singapore

## Scenario

**Profile**: Foreigner on Employment Pass, single, just arrived from Hong Kong. Looking to rent for 12-24 months before deciding on long-term plans. Office in Marina Bay. Budget $4,500/month rent.

## Step 1: Intake

> User: I just moved to Singapore. Looking to rent for at least a year, maybe two. Work in Marina Bay area. Budget $4.5k/month. Prefer modern condo with gym and pool. 1-bed or studio.

> Agent: Got it. EP holder, 12-24 month plan, work in MBFC area, $4.5k budget, condo with facilities, 1-bed/studio.
>
> Key things for you:
> - 12-month minimum is standard for diplomatic clause to kick in (foreigner protection if EP terminated)
> - Stamp duty on TA: 0.4% × total rent ≈ $216 (12 mo) or $432 (24 mo)
> - Deposit typically 1 month rent per year of lease (1 mo for 1 yr, 2 mo for 2 yr) plus 1 month advance rent
> - Total upfront for 24-month lease at $4.5k: ~$13.5k ($4.5k advance + $9k deposit + ~$432 stamp + agent commission if you engage one)

## Step 2: Area shortlist for Marina Bay commute

For $4.5k with facilities: River Valley, Tanjong Pagar, Outram Park, Tiong Bahru likely sweet spot.

## Step 3: Shortlist filters

- 1-bedroom or studio, 450-650 sqft
- Condo with pool, gym, security
- Newer building (≤ 15 yrs) for modern fittings
- Walking distance (< 10 min) to MRT
- Furnished (you're new, no furniture)
- Diplomatic clause acceptable

## Step 5: TA review checklist

Before signing the Tenancy Agreement:

- [ ] Diplomatic clause (12-month minimum, 2-month notice, requires EP termination proof)
- [ ] Minor repair cap (commonly $150-$200, tenant pays first)
- [ ] Aircon servicing (quarterly, tenant pays; verify cost ~$100/visit for 3 units)
- [ ] Inventory list with photos, signed by both parties on handover
- [ ] Reinstatement clause (return unit in original condition allowing for fair wear and tear)
- [ ] Joint-and-several liability (if any co-tenant)
- [ ] Pets clause (if you'll have pets)
- [ ] Renewal clause (any pre-agreed terms?)
- [ ] Stamp duty filed and certificate provided (legal protection)
- [ ] Utilities account in your name (SP Group + PUB)
- [ ] Wi-Fi: check existing contract; transfer or new account

## Step 6: Tenant verification (you, by landlord)

Landlord will ask for:
- Passport
- Employment Pass card
- Employment letter (offer or current company letter)
- Last 3 months payslips
- Sometimes: previous landlord reference

## Step 7: Move-in logistics

- Inventory walk-through with agent and landlord on Day 1
- Document existing damage with date-stamped photos
- Test: all aircons (cooling + condensation pan), water pressure, locks, lights, appliances
- Confirm Wi-Fi and TV credentials transferred
- Take meter readings (electricity, water, gas if any)
- Update IRAS rental address (for tax mailings)
- Update bank, immigration (ICA), and HR with new address

## Step 8: Tax considerations for tenant

- Stamp duty on TA: tenant typically pays (~$216 for 12-month at $4.5k)
- No income tax implications for tenant
- Property tax is landlord's problem, not yours

## Step 9: Renewal vs move

After 12 months, landlord usually offers renewal:
- Market rate basis (compare current listings in same project)
- Negotiate at market; if landlord asks > 10% increase, push back with data
- Or move: factor in moving cost (~$500-$1,500) and disruption

## Step 10: Escalation

- CEA agent (you can engage your own; standard fee 1 month rent for >12-month lease, often split or seller-paid in tight market)
- For TA review questions: small claims tribunal if dispute escalates (post-handover)
- CASE for general consumer protection

End: "All figures illustrative; rental market moves quickly. Confirm current asking with the agent and recent transacted rentals in the project."
