# Example 8: Condo to HDB Downgrade (15-Month Wait-Out)

## Scenario A: Couple under 55 (full wait-out applies)

**Profile**: Married couple, both SC, both 48. Own a $1.6M condo in Punggol (bought 2017, outstanding loan $400k, CPF used + accrued ~$300k). Both work but stress, want simpler life, kids in secondary school. Considering 5-room HDB resale in Sengkang.

### Step 1: The brutal truth

> Agent: Before anything else: the **15-month wait-out** applies fully to you.
>
> Since Sep 30, 2022, private property owners must wait **15 months after disposing of private property** before buying a non-subsidised HDB resale flat.
>
> No senior exemption for you (need both spouses 55+).
>
> Implication: sell condo → wait 15 months in alternative housing → then buy HDB.
>
> Three viable approaches:
> 1. **Standard path**: sell, rent for 15 months, buy HDB
> 2. **Appeal**: file appeal with HDB if you have extenuating circumstances (~25% success rate)
> 3. **Defer**: keep condo for another 15 months, then sell when you're closer to HDB-ready

## Step 2: Standard path math

Sell condo:
| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Sale price | $1,600,000 |
| Less outstanding mortgage | -$400,000 |
| Less agent commission (2% + GST) | -$34,880 |
| Less legal | -$2,800 |
| Less SSD | $0 (held 9 years) |
| **Net before CPF refund** | **$1,162,320** |
| Less CPF refund | -$300,000 |
| **Net cash** | **$862,320** |

15-month interim rental:
- HDB 4-room rental in Sengkang: ~$3,000/mo
- 15 months × $3,000 = **$45,000**
- Plus moving (twice): ~$2,500

Cash position after wait: $862,320 − $47,500 = **$814,820**

Buy 5-room HDB resale at $720k:
- BSD: $16,200
- ABSD: $0
- 5% cash deposit: $36,000
- 20% down payment: $144,000 (CPF + cash)
- Bank loan or HDB loan: $540,000
- Legal: $2,800

Cash needed: ~$200,000 (using CPF where possible)
Cash position post-purchase: $814,820 − $200,000 (rough) = **$614,820 liquid**

Plus $300,000 CPF returned + future bank loan paid via CPF.

### Step 3: Income ceiling check for HDB

> Agent: One more critical check. To take an **HDB loan** (cheaper rate, more flexible than bank), household income ≤ $14,000/month.
>
> Above $14k: must use bank loan. Bank loan rates currently ~2.5-3%, vs HDB loan ~2.6%.
>
> Also: grant eligibility cuts off at $14k. If you qualify for EHG / Family Grant: nice bonus.
>
> What's your combined household income?

User: $13,500/month.

> Within ceiling. You can use HDB loan AND apply for grants. Eligibility for:
> - Family Grant (Resale): $50k for 5-room (income ≤ $14k, family scheme)
> - EHG: graduated by income; at $13.5k, around $5-10k
> - **Wait** — grants for second-time buyers are at half rate if you previously received CPF housing grants. Did you take any on your condo? Or earlier flat?

User: First-time HDB; condo was first property.

> Then full grant rates apply. Family Grant $50k + EHG ~$10k = **~$60k in grants** = effectively reduces your HDB price by $60k.

## Step 4: Reframe with grants

- HDB price $720k − $60k grants (paid into CPF OA on completion) = effective $660k
- Lower CPF outlay on completion since grants offset
- Net cash + CPF position after purchase: even healthier

### Step 5: Appeal path (if you have grounds)

> Agent: Should you appeal the wait-out? Considerations:
>
> Successful appeals typically involve:
> - Documentary proof of OTP **before Sep 30, 2022** — doesn't apply to you
> - Extenuating circumstances: medical, elder care, financial hardship
> - HDB-approved special schemes
>
> Your case (general lifestyle downsize) is unlikely to succeed. Approval rate ~25%, mostly the above categories.
>
> Recommendation: don't bank on appeal. Plan for the 15-month wait.

### Step 6: Alternative — defer

> Agent: Have you considered holding the condo for another year or two while you wait it out anyway?
>
> Math: keep condo, continue paying mortgage. After 15 months, sell condo → immediately apply to buy HDB resale (still triggers the wait from sale completion).
>
> Actually no — the 15-month wait runs from **disposal completion**, not from purchase decision. Keeping the condo doesn't help; you still wait 15 months from sale.
>
> Unless: you keep condo, rent it out for the 15 months, and don't move into HDB until ~18 months from now anyway. Doesn't change the wait but changes your living situation:
>
> - Stay in condo as own-stay
> - List it for sale 15 months before desired HDB move-in date
> - Time the sale to complete 15 months before HDB OTP
> - Live in interim rental for the gap, OR
> - Coordinate so sale completion + 15 months = HDB OTP date
>
> This is just timing the same wait differently. End result similar.

### Step 7: Recommended path

> Standard sell-and-wait. Budget:
> - 15-month rental: $45k
> - Two moves: $2.5k
> - Total interim cost: $47.5k
>
> Net outcome:
> - Live in HDB with manageable monthly costs (~$2,000-$2,500 total)
> - $600k+ liquid post-purchase
> - $300k+ CPF returned
> - $60k grants
> - Mortgage tenure shorter, lower stress

## Scenario B: Couple aged 55+ (senior exemption)

**Profile**: Same as above but both spouses are 58 and 56. Looking at a 4-room HDB resale.

### Step 1: Eligibility check

> Agent: Wait — both spouses 55+? Buying 4-room or smaller? You qualify for the **senior exemption**.
>
> No wait-out. You can sell condo and immediately apply for HDB resale.
>
> Senior exemption covers:
> - 4-room or smaller resale flat, OR
> - 2-room Flexi flat, OR
> - Community Care Apartment (CCA) intended for seniors
>
> Both spouses must be 55+ at time of application.

### Step 2: Direct transition timeline

| Month | Action |
|-------|--------|
| 0 | List condo, concurrent HDB search |
| 2-3 | Condo OTP |
| 3 | HDB OTP (can be concurrent now) |
| 4-5 | Condo completion |
| 5-7 | HDB resale completion |
| 7 | Move from condo to HDB |

**Total**: ~7 months. Saves 18+ months and ~$50k in interim rental.

### Step 3: Senior-specific HDB considerations

- **Income ceiling** still applies for HDB loan and grants (assume retired or partial; verify)
- **CPF housing grants** for seniors: still available if first-time HDB applicant (or half-rate if previously subsidised)
- **2-room Flexi**: short-lease option (15-45 years) optimised for seniors; cheaper, suitable for couples with no children to bequeath to
- **CCA**: assisted living designed for seniors aged 65+
- **Silver Housing Bonus**: cash bonus for downsize from condo/HDB to smaller HDB (up to $30k); criteria includes household monthly income at time of purchase

## Step 5: Final word

> Agent: For your specific situation (under 55 in scenario A): expect 26 months total. Front-load planning for interim housing. Use the 15 months to: pay down condo loan further if you're holding, scope HDB choices, build interim savings, or take a sabbatical-style life event.
>
> For senior path (B): direct transition. Major financial restructure with strong retirement upside.
