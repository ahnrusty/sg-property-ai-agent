# Example 7: Landed to Condo Downsize (Empty Nesters, Retirement)

## Scenario

**Profile**: Married couple, both SC, both 62 years old. Own a fully-paid $5.5M freehold semi-detached in Serangoon (held 22 years). 3 adult children all moved out. CPF used + accrued interest on landed: combined ~$650k. Want to downsize to a 3-bedroom condo in River Valley for ~$2.8M.

**Goal**: Unlock equity, simplify maintenance, optimise retirement income.

## Step 1: Triage

> Agent: Classic landed downsize. Key advantages:
>
> 1. **No SSD** (held 22 years, way past 3-year window)
> 2. **No 15-month wait-out** (only applies if going to HDB)
> 3. **Senior CPF benefit**: refund first tops up RA to FRS, then balance to OA
> 4. **No ABSD on condo** if you sell landed first (or within 6-month remission window)
>
> Two paths:
>
> **Path A**: Sell landed first, buy condo (clean, no ABSD)
> **Path B**: Buy condo first, sell landed within 6 months (continuity, but requires $560k ABSD upfront)
>
> Path A is the obvious choice at 62 — you have time, no rush.

## Step 2: Sell landed math

Agent runs the numbers:

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Landed sale | $5,500,000 |
| Less outstanding mortgage | $0 (paid off) |
| Less agent commission (2% + 9% GST) | -$119,900 |
| Less legal fees | -$3,500 |
| Less SSD | $0 (held > 3 years) |
| **Net before CPF refund** | **$5,376,600** |
| Less CPF refund (principal + accrued interest, combined) | -$650,000 |
| **Net cash to seller** | **$4,726,600** |

**CPF refund treatment** (both spouses 62, past age 55):
- Each spouse's CPF refund first tops up Retirement Account to **FRS $220,400 (2026 figure)**
- Any excess flows to OA
- If RA is already at FRS, all refund goes to OA
- Result: significant RA top-up for both, boosting future CPF LIFE monthly payouts for life

> Agent: "Your refund is split between both your CPF accounts based on your original contributions to the property. Assume roughly even split for planning: ~$325k each.
>
> If your RA isn't already at FRS, the first ~$220k of each spouse's refund tops up RA. Remaining ~$105k each goes to OA.
>
> RA top-up effect on CPF LIFE: every $100k in RA roughly adds $500-600/month for life starting at age 70 (CPF LIFE Standard plan). Both spouses topped up to FRS = combined ~$2,000-2,400/month for life on top of any existing payouts."

## Step 3: Buy condo math ($2.8M)

Agent calls:
- `calculate_bsd(2_800_000)`: 1%×180 + 2%×180 + 3%×640 + 4%×500 + 5%×1,300 = 1,800 + 3,600 + 19,200 + 20,000 + 65,000 = **$109,600**
- `calculate_absd(2_800_000, "SC", 1)`: **$0** (no other properties after landed sale)

Funding options (at 62 with $4.7M cash):
- **Full cash purchase** (recommended for retirees): no mortgage, no interest, simpler
- **Partial loan** (LTV up to 75%, but borrower age caps): max tenure = `min(30, 75-62)` = 13 years for age-cap reasons

Let's do full cash:

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Condo purchase | $2,800,000 |
| BSD | $109,600 |
| Legal | $3,500 |
| Stamp duty admin + valuation | $1,000 |
| Renovation / move-in | ~$50,000 (estimate) |
| **Total outlay** | **$2,964,100** |

**Liquid cash remaining**: $4,726,600 − $2,964,100 = **$1,762,500**

Plus boosted RA → higher CPF LIFE payouts for life.

## Step 4: Retirement income picture

After downsize:

| Source | Approx monthly |
|--------|----------------|
| CPF LIFE (existing + RA top-up boost) | $4,500 - $5,500 |
| Investment income on $1.76M liquid (assume 4% net) | ~$5,900 |
| Optional: rental income if condo rented in future | TBD |
| **Total** | **~$10,000 - $11,500/month for life** |

Plus paid-off home, no mortgage stress.

## Step 5: Condo selection for retirees

Criteria typically:
- Single-level living (no internal stairs)
- Lift access (avoid walk-up apartments)
- Modern fittings, low maintenance
- Good security
- Pool and gym (gentle exercise)
- Walking distance to MRT and amenities
- Hospital and clinics nearby
- Quiet stack (avoid pool deck or BBQ area)
- Furnished or move-in ready (avoid major reno at this life stage)

For River Valley area (D9/D10):
- The Anson Park, Robertson Quay, Martin Modern, Avenue South Residence, Riviere, M5
- Typical 3BR: 1,200-1,500 sqft, $1,900-$2,500 psf
- $2.8M budget targets sweet spot mid-segment

## Step 6: Sequencing timeline

| Week | Action |
|------|--------|
| W1 | List landed with exclusive agent (3-month) |
| W1-W4 | Concurrent: shortlist condo options |
| W2-W6 | Landed viewings; serious buyer interest |
| W6-W8 | Landed OTP signed; exercise within 14 days |
| W8-W12 | Condo OTP signed (timing post-landed sale to avoid ABSD) |
| W12-W20 | Landed completion (8-12 weeks); condo completion (overlapping) |
| W18-W22 | Move from landed to condo (single move with bridging week if needed) |
| W22+ | CPF refund credited; RA top-ups confirmed; surplus to OA |

If a 1-2 week gap: stay with adult children, hotel, or short serviced apartment.

## Step 7: Pre-execution checklist

- [ ] Update wills to reflect simplified estate
- [ ] CPF nominations refreshed
- [ ] Healthcare insurance review (downsize may free cashflow for better coverage)
- [ ] Investment plan for $1.76M liquid surplus (low-risk income-generating)
- [ ] Discuss with adult children: family heirlooms, photos, furniture
- [ ] Storage: garage / lawn equipment / large furniture you won't take
- [ ] Donate or sell items not needed in condo
- [ ] Update IRAS for property tax change
- [ ] Notify GP, banks, utilities of address change

## Step 8: Tax and estate considerations

- **No capital gains tax** on the landed sale (Singapore advantage)
- **Property tax**: condo will have lower AV than landed; lower annual property tax
- **Estate**: with $4.7M+ in liquid + boosted CPF + condo, refresh wills with lawyer
- **Family trust**: consider for estate efficiency if significant assets
- **CPF LIFE plan choice**: at 65, you'll be auto-included in CPF LIFE Standard. Consider Escalating Plan if you expect long lifespan and want inflation protection

## Step 9: Escalation

- **CEA agent** for landed (premium property; experienced agent matters)
- **Conveyancing lawyer** for both transactions (one firm OK for buy-then-sell efficiency)
- **Independent financial planner** for the $1.7M surplus
- **CPF Board** for RA top-up confirmation and CPF LIFE planning
- **Estate planning lawyer** for wills, trusts, lasting power of attorney

End: "Retirement downsize is one of the most impactful financial moves you'll make. Take 2-3 months to plan thoroughly; the timing pressure is low and the upside is enormous."
