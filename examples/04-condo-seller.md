# Example 4: Selling a Private Condo

## Scenario

**Profile**: SC homeowner, sold first home 4 years ago, currently owns a 99-year leasehold 3BR condo in Bishan, bought in 2019 for $1.45M. Outstanding mortgage $620k. CPF used on this property + accrued interest: ~$280k. Asking $1.85M.

**Goal**: Sell to upgrade. Want to understand net proceeds and SSD risk.

## Step 1: SSD check

> Agent: Bought 2019, selling 2026. Held > 3 years → **no SSD** (`calculate_ssd(1_850_000, 84)` confirms 0).
>
> No tax drag on the sale.

## Step 2: Net proceeds estimate

> Agent breaks down:

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Sale price (asking) | $1,850,000 |
| Less: outstanding mortgage | -$620,000 |
| Less: CPF refund (principal + accrued interest) | -$280,000 |
| Less: agent commission 2% + 9% GST | -$40,330 |
| Less: legal fees | -$2,800 |
| Less: any redemption penalty (if within lock-in) | $0 (assume out of lock-in) |
| **Net cash to seller** | **~$906,870** |
| **CPF returned to OA** | **$280,000** |

> Of which $906k is liquid cash and $280k sits back in your OA earning 2.5%.

## Step 3: Pricing sanity check

> Pull recent transactions in the project (last 12 months):
> Agent suggests using URA Realis filtered to same project, same size band, same age band.
>
> Compute median $/psf for comparable units. Asking $1.85M / 1,100 sqft = $1,682/psf.
>
> If median is $1,650, asking is at the 60th percentile — defensible.
> If median is $1,500, asking is at the 90th percentile — over-priced; expect to negotiate down.

## Step 4: Listing strategy

> - Exclusive agent or open listing? Exclusive typically: 1 agent runs the listing aggressively, you control narrative. 3-month exclusive is common.
> - Photography: professional photos (~$300-$500) increase view-through rates ~3x.
> - Listing portals: PropertyGuru + 99.co + EdgeProp — agent typically handles.
> - Staging: declutter, deep clean, neutral paint touch-ups, fresh flowers on viewing days.
> - Open house: weekend afternoons; 2-3 sessions in first 2 weeks.

## Step 5: Buyer pool

> 99-year leasehold (~76 years left), 3BR, Bishan = good schools nearby (Catholic High, Raffles Inst., Whitley Sec).
>
> Likely buyer profiles:
> - HDB upgrader couple with school-age kids → strong demand
> - Investor seeking rental income (Bishan rental ~$5-6k/mo, gross yield ~3.5%)
> - Family from CCR/RCR moving for school catchment
>
> Limited foreign buyer demand (60% ABSD).

## Step 6: Reasonable offers vs lowball

> Anchor: ask agent for serious offers in writing only.
>
> Range expectation:
> - First serious offer: typically 5-10% below asking ($1.67M-$1.76M)
> - Negotiation landing: 3-5% below ask ($1.76M-$1.79M)
> - Hold the line if multiple offers; raise after 2 weeks of listing if no traction

## Step 7: Counter-offer playbook

| Buyer offer | Response |
|-------------|----------|
| $1.65M (11% below) | Counter at $1.83M; explain transacted comparable |
| $1.78M (3.9% below) | Counter at $1.82M-$1.83M; close the gap |
| $1.85M (at ask) | Accept immediately; check buyer's IPA |

## Step 8: OTP process (seller side)

1. Accept offer in writing
2. Engage lawyer (provide buyer's lawyer details for liaison)
3. Issue OTP; receive 1% option fee from buyer ($18,500)
4. Buyer has 14 days to exercise; pays 4% balance to total 5% deposit
5. Stamp duties (BSD, ABSD) — buyer's responsibility
6. Loan disbursement — buyer's bank to seller's lawyer
7. Completion typically 8-12 weeks after OTP
8. Hand over keys, access cards, manuals, warranties, MCST contact

## Step 9: Buying next home — sequence

> If buying next home (upgrader path):
> - Option A: List condo now, buy next home after sale completes (clean, but bridging rental ~$3-5k/mo for 2-3 months)
> - Option B: Buy first, sell within 6 months for ABSD remission. Requires bridging cash for ABSD ($460k+ on $2.3M next home) and bridging loan.
> - Option C: Sell condo and bridge into next purchase with conditional OTP timing.
>
> Plan with lawyer and banker before listing.

## Step 10: Escalation

- CEA-registered seller's agent (2% commission negotiable)
- Conveyancing lawyer ($2,500-$3,500 for residential)
- Bank for redemption process (3-month notice often required, or pay penalty)
- Tax adviser if any unusual claims (none here, standard sale)

End: "All net figures illustrative. Confirm CPF accrued interest with CPF Board (My CPF dashboard) and agent commission with the engagement letter."
