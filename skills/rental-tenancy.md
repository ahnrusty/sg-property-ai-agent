# Rental and Tenancy

For tenants (renting) and landlords (letting out).

## Lease terms

- **Tenure**: 12 to 24 months typical; some landlords accept 6-month for premium rent.
- **Security deposit**: 1 month per year of lease (1 mth for 1 yr, 2 mths for 2 yrs).
- **Advance rent**: 1 month payable on lease signing.
- **Stamp duty**: 0.4% of total rent for leases ≤ 4 years. Paid via IRAS e-Stamping. Usually borne by tenant.
- **Diplomatic clause**: For foreigner tenants, allows early termination after 12 months minimum stay with 2 months' written notice and proof of relocation/work termination.
- **Minor repair cap**: Tenant bears repair cost up to a fixed amount per incident (commonly $150 to $200); landlord bears the excess. Typical for: aircon servicing, plumbing fixes, minor electrical.
- **Aircon servicing**: Quarterly servicing usually tenant's responsibility. Document who pays.
- **Inventory list**: Detailed list with photos signed by both parties at handover.

## Tenant checklist

- [ ] Verify landlord's title via SLA INLIS or request title search through your agent.
- [ ] Inspect unit and document existing wear with date-stamped photos.
- [ ] Test all appliances, lights, taps, locks, aircons.
- [ ] Confirm utilities account transfer arrangement (your name vs landlord's).
- [ ] Diplomatic clause language clear if foreigner.
- [ ] Stamp duty paid and certificate obtained (legal protection in dispute).
- [ ] Read TA carefully; ask for specific changes before signing (not after).
- [ ] Joint-and-several liability clause: understand it (each tenant fully liable for full rent).
- [ ] Subletting clause: usually prohibited.
- [ ] Pet clause: explicit permission if you have pets.
- [ ] Reinstatement clause: what condition you must return the unit in.
- [ ] Renewal clause: any pre-agreed renewal terms.

## Landlord checklist

- [ ] Tenant verification: passport, work pass, employment letter, NRIC for SC/SPR.
- [ ] Income proof (3 months payslips, employment letter).
- [ ] Reference check (previous landlord if possible).
- [ ] Stamp duty filed (if borne by you, otherwise confirm tenant has filed).
- [ ] Inventory list signed and photo-documented.
- [ ] Insurance: homeowner's policy covers some landlord liability; consider landlord-specific policy.
- [ ] HDB whole-flat sublet approval (if HDB).
- [ ] Condo MCST permits rental (check minimum tenure clauses in by-laws).
- [ ] Income tax: rental income is taxable (less allowable deductions).
- [ ] Annual filing of rental income on IRAS.

## HDB renting

| Scenario | Allowed |
|----------|---------|
| Renting out whole HDB flat | Only after MOP; HDB approval required; 6 to 36 months for SC tenants, 6 to 24 months for non-citizens |
| Renting out bedroom(s) only | Allowed during MOP for 3-room and larger; owner must continue to live in flat |
| Renting to companies for staff housing | Allowed but tenant must be individuals on TA, capped on number of occupants |

Maximum occupants per HDB flat:
- 1-room: 2 persons
- 2-room: 4 persons
- 3-room: 6 persons
- 4-room and larger: 6 persons

Subletting without HDB approval can result in HDB compulsorily acquiring the flat. Penalty significant.

## Condo renting

- MCST may impose minimum tenure (commonly 3 months due to URA rules).
- Short-term rental (< 3 months) is banned for residential property by URA (no Airbnb).
- By-laws may restrict number of occupants, pets, vehicles.
- Inform MCST and security of new tenant details for access card issuance.

## Rental yield (landlord view)

Gross yield = annual rent ÷ purchase price.

Net yield: deduct annual maintenance, property tax (non-owner-occupied rate, much higher), insurance, agent fee, repairs reserve, vacancy buffer (typically 1 month per year).

Singapore condo gross yields (2026 indicative):
- Mass-market OCR: 3.0 to 4.0%
- City fringe RCR: 2.8 to 3.5%
- Prime CCR: 2.5 to 3.2%
- Older leasehold or city fringe: 3.5 to 5.0% (lease decay drag)

Property tax for non-owner-occupied is significantly higher than owner-occupied; factor this into net yield.

## Diplomatic clause typical wording

> Notwithstanding any provision herein to the contrary, in the event that the Tenant or one of the Tenants shall (i) cease to be employed in Singapore or be transferred out of Singapore, or (ii) be denied an Employment Pass or extension thereof by the relevant authority, the Tenant shall be entitled to terminate this Tenancy by giving the Landlord not less than two (2) months' written notice or two (2) months' rent in lieu of notice, such notice not to be served before the expiry of twelve (12) months from the date of commencement of this Tenancy. Documentary evidence of relocation or work termination must be provided.

## Renewal and rental adjustment

- Typical renewal: 60 days before expiry, landlord proposes renewal rent.
- Market rate basis: compare to similar units' new rentals.
- Negotiation leverage: long-tenured tenant vs vacancy risk.
- If landlord asks for > 10% increase: provide market data; counter at market rate.

## Dispute and resolution

- **Small Claims Tribunals (SCT)**: For claims up to $20,000 (or $30,000 with agreement). Cheap and fast.
- **Consumer Association of Singapore (CASE)**: Mediation services.
- **Council for Estate Agencies (CEA)**: Complaints against agents.

Document everything in writing throughout the tenancy. Email and WhatsApp are admissible evidence.

## Stamp duty on tenancy agreement

| Average rent | Stamp duty |
|--------------|-------------|
| ≤ $1,000/month | $1 |
| > $1,000/month | 0.4% of total rent over lease period |

Filed via IRAS e-Stamping. Late filing penalty up to 4x duty. Tenant typically pays unless otherwise agreed.
