# Private Condo Due Diligence

For private non-landed condos and apartments (resale and new launch).

## Title and ownership

- Pull title search via your conveyancing lawyer (~$50 to $80 to SLA).
- Verify seller name matches IC or company UEN.
- Check for caveats, mortgages, charges, and any pending litigation.
- For company-owned units, request to see the latest ACRA bizfile and director resolutions authorising the sale.

## Tenure

| Type | Implication |
|------|-------------|
| Freehold | No expiry. Premium pricing. Better for very long hold or generational. |
| 999-year leasehold | Effectively freehold for any human horizon. |
| 99-year leasehold | Decays per Bala's Curve. Watch CPF and bank loan implications under 60 years. |

For 99-year leasehold, compute remaining lease at expected completion date and at expected sale date. Plan accordingly.

## MCST and management

- **Management Corporation Strata Title (MCST)** is the legal entity that runs the development.
- Request:
  - Last 2 AGM minutes (financial position, special projects, disputes)
  - Annual financial statements (income vs expenditure, reserves)
  - Sinking fund balance and 5-year capex forecast
  - Any pending or recent litigation
- High turnover of managing agents or chair = potential governance issues.

## Maintenance fees and sinking fund

- Monthly maintenance: typically $250 to $500 for mass-market, $500 to $1,200 for luxury, $1,500+ for ultra-luxury.
- Sinking fund: portion of maintenance set aside for major works (roof, lift overhaul, façade painting). A development of 20+ years should have a substantial sinking fund or be planning to top up via special levy.
- Special levies: ask about any pending or recently approved special levies. A surprise $5,000 to $20,000 special levy after purchase is unpleasant.

## Common area condition

Walk the development at different times:

- Weekday morning: maintenance crew quality, lobby cleanliness
- Weekday evening: lighting, security, lift performance
- Weekend afternoon: facility usage, noise levels
- Weekend night: parking pressure, nuisance from BBQ pits

Check:

- Pool (water clarity, equipment age, deck repair)
- Gym (equipment age and condition)
- Lifts (number, response time, last servicing notice)
- Security (guardhouse, CCTV coverage, access cards)
- Landscaping (overgrown = under-budgeted maintenance)
- Carpark (resident vs visitor lots, EV charging readiness)

## Unit-specific

| Item | Check |
|------|-------|
| Leaks | Ceilings, balcony, around windows, under sinks |
| Water marks | Common in older RC slab; can indicate upstairs leak |
| Cracks | Hairline ok; structural cracks need engineer assessment |
| Windows | Frames, hinges, weatherstrips, sliding mechanism |
| Aircon | Compressor age, ledge accessibility, gas top-up history |
| Balcony | Tile lippage, drain blockage, glass railing seal |
| Floor | Parquet condition, marble polishing, tile lippage |
| Kitchen | Worktop chips, cabinet hinges, hood ventilation |
| Bathrooms | Grout, sealant, water pressure, drainage |
| Electrical | Sockets, switches, DB panel age, RCD presence |
| Soundproofing | Test by closing all windows; listen for road, MRT, neighbour |

## Stack and facing

- Sun: north / south facing avoids direct east-west sun. West-facing afternoons are hot.
- Wind: corner stacks usually have better cross-ventilation.
- View: high floor is not always better (lower floors can have garden view; high floors can have road or train line views).
- Privacy: facing another tower's living rooms = privacy issue.
- Noise: facing pool, BBQ pit, MRT line, or expressway = ongoing nuisance.

## By-laws

Request the MCST by-laws. Common rules to check:

- Pets: small dogs may be allowed; HDB rules do not apply
- Renovation hours: typical 9 am to 5 pm weekdays, restricted weekends
- Short-term rental: typically banned (URA requires minimum 3-month lease)
- Vehicle types: motorbikes, vans, commercial vehicles
- Use of common areas: pool reservation, function room booking

## En-bloc history

For older developments (15+ years), check:

- Any past en-bloc attempts and why they failed
- Current Collective Sale Committee (CSC) status
- Reserve price expectations
- Owner sentiment (talk to neighbours during viewing)

If you intend to hold for 10+ years, en-bloc potential can be upside. If you intend to live there for 30+ years, en-bloc disrupts your plan.

## Rental yield (for investment buys)

| Yield band | What it means |
|------------|---------------|
| < 3% | Capital appreciation play, weak yield |
| 3 to 4% | Typical Singapore prime |
| 4 to 5% | Above-average, often older or city-fringe |
| > 5% | Investigate; could be poor area, dated unit, or distressed |

Compute net yield: annual rent − (maintenance + property tax + insurance + agent fee + vacancy buffer + repairs reserve) ÷ purchase price.

## Future development risk

Pull URA Master Plan for the immediate area:

- Planned MRT lines (positive for value but disruptive during construction)
- Planned new condos (supply pressure)
- Industrial or commercial zoning nearby
- Road widening or new expressway
- School relocation or new schools

## Red flags

- Seller refuses to provide MCST minutes
- "As-is" sale without inspection allowance
- Recent renovation hiding underlying issues (test thoroughly)
- Pricing significantly above last 6 months of comparable transactions
- Agent pressuring fast OTP signing
- Vacant unit with no story (could be problem unit)
- Multiple owners exiting the same project simultaneously (something is happening)
- En-bloc EGM in next 12 months and price reflects en-bloc upside (you take the risk)
