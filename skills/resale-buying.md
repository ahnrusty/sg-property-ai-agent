# Resale Buying

For purchasing already-completed private and HDB properties from the existing owner.

## Process (private resale)

1. **Search and shortlist**: PropertyGuru, 99.co, EdgeProp.
2. **Engage your own agent (optional)**: Buyer can have their own agent or deal directly with seller's agent.
3. **Viewings**: Multiple visits, different times, weekday and weekend.
4. **Verification**: Title, MCST, transacted prices, due diligence.
5. **Offer letter**: Written or WhatsApp; specify price, terms, completion timeline.
6. **OTP issued by seller**: Pay 1% option fee (cash or cashier's order).
7. **Engage lawyer and bank**: Lawyer for conveyancing, bank for loan disbursement.
8. **Exercise OTP**: Typically within 14 days. Pay 4% balance of deposit (total 5%).
9. **Stamp duties**: BSD and ABSD within 14 days of OTP.
10. **Loan and CPF arrangements**: Bank disburses loan on completion.
11. **Completion**: Typically 8 to 12 weeks after OTP. Lawyer handles title transfer, balance payment, key handover.

## Process (HDB resale)

1. **Search**: HDB Resale Portal (https://services2.hdb.gov.sg), PropertyGuru, 99.co
2. **Register Intent to Buy**: On HDB Resale Portal, free, valid 12 months. Required.
3. **Obtain HFE Letter** (HDB Flat Eligibility) **or** bank IPA.
4. **Viewings**.
5. **Offer**: Direct or through agent. Negotiate price (including any COV component).
6. **OTP issued by seller**: Option fee $1 to $1,000 (agreed).
7. **Exercise OTP**: Within 21 days. Pay balance deposit (typical total $5,000).
8. **Both parties submit Resale Application** on HDB Resale Portal within 7 days of exercise.
9. **HDB processes** (~8 weeks): includes value check, eligibility verification, ethnic quota.
10. **Both parties attend Completion Appointment** at HDB Hub.
11. **Keys handed over**.

## OTP basics

- **Option fee**: Paid when seller grants OTP. Usually 1% for private, $1 to $1,000 for HDB.
- **Option period**: Time during which buyer can decide to exercise (sign the option). Standard 14 days for private, 21 days for HDB.
- **Exercise**: Buyer signs the OTP and pays additional 4% (private) or balance deposit (HDB).
- **Total deposit**: 5% for private, $5,000 typical for HDB.
- If buyer does not exercise, option fee is forfeited (option lapses).
- If seller withdraws after granting OTP, depending on terms, may need to refund option fee with damages.

## Cash and CPF flow at OTP and exercise

| Stage | Cash | CPF |
|-------|------|-----|
| Option fee (private 1%) | Yes | No |
| Exercise top-up (4%) | Yes | No (must be cash for deposit) |
| Balance down payment to 25% | Optional | Yes (up to OA balance) |
| Mortgage (75% LTV) | Bank loan | Repaid via CPF or cash monthly |
| BSD | Yes | Yes (reimbursement) |
| ABSD | Yes | Yes for new launch; No for resale (cash only) |
| Legal fees | Yes | Up to CPF allowable cap |
| Valuation fee (~$300 to $500) | Yes | No |

## Valuation

For HDB resale, valuation is requested **after** OTP (via HDB Resale Portal). CPF and bank loan use the lower of price or valuation; any premium above valuation is COV in cash.

For private resale, the lender will do a valuation as part of loan approval. Loan quantum is 75% × min(price, valuation). Difference must be made up in cash/CPF.

## Conveyancing lawyer

- Engage when OTP is signed (some engage at offer stage for review).
- Lawyer handles: title search, replies to legal requisitions, drafting / reviewing OTP and S&P, liaising with seller's lawyer, bank, IRAS, and completing the transaction.
- Cost: $2,500 to $5,000 for residential.
- CPF-approved law firm panel exists; check with bank and CPF.

## Bank loan

- Submit IPA application with documents: 3 months payslips, 12 months bank statements, NOA, IC, CBS credit report (bank pulls).
- IPA valid 30 days typically.
- After OTP, formal Letter of Offer issued by bank.
- Accept Letter of Offer, sign mortgage documents.
- Bank disburses loan on completion.

## Insurance

- **Fire insurance**: mandatory for mortgaged property. ~$50 to $150/year for typical condo.
- **Home contents insurance**: optional; covers belongings and liability.
- **Mortgage Reducing Term Assurance (MRTA)**: optional; life insurance that pays off mortgage on death. HPS (Home Protection Scheme) is the mandatory equivalent for HDB owners using CPF.

## Completion

On completion day:

- Lawyer effects title transfer at SLA
- Bank releases loan to seller's lawyer
- Buyer pays balance via cashier's order or remittance
- Seller hands over keys, access cards, mailbox keys, manuals, warranties
- Conduct final walk-through immediately before handover; document any new issues
- Take meter readings (utilities, water)
- Update IRAS for property tax, mailing address; redirect mail

## Common resale pitfalls

- Overpaying COV without negotiating down (HDB)
- Skipping inspection of common areas and immediate neighbours
- Not factoring in renovation cost for older unit
- Underestimating timeline (8 to 12 weeks for completion + bridging period if selling current home)
- Discovering pending special levy in MCST minutes after OTP
- Buyer's own agent representing seller too (conflict of interest)
- Last-minute IPA shortfall due to debt or income variation
