# TDSR, MSR, and LTV Loan Rules

Singapore loan rules constrain how much you can borrow regardless of asset value. Plan for these before you make an offer.

## Total Debt Servicing Ratio (TDSR)

Applies to all property purchases (HDB and private). Total monthly debt obligations including the new mortgage cannot exceed **55%** of gross monthly income.

### What counts as debt

- Existing property loans (other mortgages)
- Car loans
- Renovation loans
- Credit card minimum payments (or 3% of outstanding balance, whichever is higher)
- Personal loans
- Education loans
- Any guarantee or co-borrower obligations

### Stress-test interest rate floors

The new mortgage payment is computed at a stressed rate to prevent over-leverage when rates rise.

| Property type | Stress rate floor |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Residential, bank loan | 4.0% p.a. |
| Residential, HDB loan | 3.0% p.a. |
| Non-residential | 5.0% p.a. |

The actual borrowing rate may be lower; the stress rate is used only for TDSR/MSR calculation.

### Income haircuts

| Income type | Haircut on declared income |
|-------------|----------------------------|
| Fixed salary | 0% (use 100%) |
| Variable income (commission, bonus, freelance) | 30% (use 70%) |
| Rental income | 30% (use 70%) |
| Eligible financial assets (annualised) | varies; consult bank |

## Mortgage Servicing Ratio (MSR)

Applies **only** to HDB flats and Executive Condominiums purchased from the developer. Mortgage payment alone cannot exceed **30%** of gross monthly income, computed at the same stress rate as TDSR. Both TDSR and MSR must pass.

## Loan-to-Value (LTV) limits

### Bank loans (private property and HDB resale via bank)

| Outstanding housing loans | LTV cap (loan tenure ≤ 30 yrs, lease covers borrower to age 65) | Min cash component |
|---------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------|
| 0 (first loan) | 75% | 5% (of which 5% is min cash; rest can be CPF) |
| 1 outstanding | 45% | 25% |
| 2 or more outstanding | 35% | 25% |

If loan tenure > 30 years (or extends past borrower age 65), LTV drops to 55% / 25% / 15% respectively.

### HDB loan (HFE letter)

- Up to **75%** LTV (recently reduced from 80%; verify current cap on HDB website).
- Eligibility: at least one SC buyer, household monthly income within ceiling, no ownership of private property in last 30 months, no prior HDB loan or fewer than the allowed taken.

## Maximum loan tenure

| Property type | Max tenure | Hard cap |
|---------------|------------|----------|
| HDB, HDB loan | 25 years | up to age 65 |
| HDB, bank loan | 25 years | up to age 75 |
| Private, bank loan | 30 years | up to age 75 |

If you exceed tenure or age caps, the LTV cap drops as above.

## Worked example

**Profile**: 40 yrs old, gross monthly income $12,000. No other debt. Buying a $1.5M private resale, first property.

**Stress payment**: at 4% over 30 years, monthly = $7,162 per $1M = $7,162 × 1.5 × 0.75 (assume 75% LTV = $1.125M loan) = $5,372.

Wait, recalc: $1.125M loan @ 4% over 30 yrs ≈ $5,370/mo.

**TDSR test**: $5,370 ÷ $12,000 = 44.7%. Within 55%. Passes.

**MSR**: not applicable (private property).

**LTV**: $1.5M × 75% = $1.125M loan. Cash + CPF down payment = $375k. Of which at least 5% ($75k) must be in cash; remainder can be CPF OA.

**Stamp duty cash outlay**: BSD on $1.5M = $44,600. No ABSD (first property, SC). Add lawyer ~$3.5k, valuation ~$500, fire insurance.

## Variable income earners

If you have commission, bonus, freelance, or rental income, banks will haircut it ~30%. Be prepared:

- 2 years of NOA (income tax notice of assessment)
- 6 months CPF contribution history
- 12 months bank statements
- Latest 3 months of payslips

For self-employed or directors of own company, banks may haircut further or require additional documentation.

## Common LTV traps

- Buying second property while still holding first → 45% LTV unless first is sold and proceeds cleared
- Joint borrower has existing loan → counts as outstanding for LTV ladder
- Spouse not on loan but on title → still counts for ABSD profile (highest rate applies)
- Renovation loan, car loan, or credit card balance → eats into TDSR headroom

## Refinancing rules

- Refinance to same lender or different lender; TDSR re-tested at new package
- TDSR can be waived for refinancing of owner-occupied property if you are not cashing out (consult bank)
- Lock-in period and clawback clauses apply; usually 2 to 3 years
- Legal fee subsidy and rebates are common during refinancing promotions
