# Landed Property

Terrace, semi-detached, detached, bungalow, GCB (Good Class Bungalow), and strata landed.

## Types

| Type | Typical land size (sqft) | Notes |
|------|--------------------------|-------|
| Terrace (intermediate) | 1,500–2,500 | Two party walls |
| Terrace (corner) | 2,000–3,500 | One party wall, side garden |
| Semi-detached | 2,500–4,500 | One party wall |
| Detached | 4,000–8,000 | Standalone, all setbacks |
| Bungalow | 5,000–10,000+ | Larger detached, usually freehold |
| GCB | ≥ 1,400 sqm (15,069 sqft) | Designated areas (Tanglin, Bukit Timah, Holland, etc.); strict planning rules |
| Strata landed | varies | In a strata development; landed unit within a condo-style scheme |

## Foreigner ownership

Under the Residential Property Act:

- Foreigners (including Singapore PRs) **cannot** buy landed property in mainland Singapore without prior approval from the Land Dealings Approval Unit (LDAU) of SLA.
- Approval is granted at discretion based on economic contribution to Singapore. Process takes weeks to months.
- **Sentosa Cove** is the only landed area where foreigners can buy a single landed property for own occupation without LDAU approval.
- **Singapore PRs** can apply to LDAU but approval is rare and requires meaningful economic contribution.
- Strata landed in a condo development is treated as **non-landed** for the purpose of foreigner ownership rules: foreigners can buy.

Citizens of FTA partners (Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway, Switzerland, USA) get ABSD parity with SC but **do not** get an automatic landed ownership exemption.

## Tenure

- Most landed in central Singapore is freehold or 999-year.
- Many newer landed estates (post-1990) are 99-year leasehold.
- Lease decay applies to leasehold landed: factor remaining lease into rebuild ROI.

## Plot ratio and GFA potential

URA Master Plan specifies allowable GFA. For landed, typically:

- Terrace, semi-D, detached: 1.4x plot ratio with height limit (typically 2 to 3 storeys + attic + basement)
- GCB: 1.4x plot ratio, strict 2-storey + attic + basement, generous setbacks

Rebuild and AA&A (Additions, Alterations & Amendments) submissions to URA can dramatically increase value if your purchase is an older, under-built property on good land.

## Conservation

Some areas (Joo Chiat, Blair Plain, parts of Cairnhill) have conservation status. Demolition and major façade changes are restricted. Rebuild ROI calculations should account for the constraints.

## Setback and party-wall rules

- Front setback: typically 2.4 m or more (corner lots more)
- Side setback: 2 m for detached, less for semi-D and terrace
- Party walls: terrace and semi-D share party walls; reconstruction requires neighbour cooperation

## Soil and structural

- Heavy clay soils (parts of Bukit Timah, Holland) → piling expensive
- Reclaimed land → check soil report
- Slopes and retaining walls → engineering costs
- Existing house age > 30 years → likely needs reconstruction not just AA&A

## Plot value vs house value

For older landed, much of the price is **land value**. The house may be tear-down. Compute:

> Implied land $/psf = (purchase price − rebuild cost saved) ÷ land area

Compare implied land $/psf to recent transactions on similar plots. Be wary of paying for the house if you intend to demolish.

## Rebuild cost guide (2026 estimates)

- Standard build, terrace / semi-D: $350 to $500 psf GFA
- Mid-spec detached: $500 to $700 psf
- Luxury bungalow / GCB: $800 to $1,500 psf GFA
- Basement: typically $1,200 to $1,800 psf for the basement portion alone

Add 12 to 24 months of construction time, design fees (5 to 10% of construction), and contingency (10%).

## Property tax (annual)

Landed has higher annual value (AV) than apartments of similar transacted value, leading to higher property tax. For owner-occupied:

- AV ≤ $12,000: 0%
- $12,001 to $30,000: 4%
- Progressive bands up to 32% for AV > $140,000

For non-owner-occupied (rented out or vacant), 12 to 36% bands apply. Verify current bands on IRAS.

## Due diligence specifics

- Plot survey and as-built drawings
- URA gross plot ratio and any encroachments
- Drainage reserve, road reserve, MRT tunnel reserve underground
- Underground utilities (PUB, SP Group, NetLink)
- Tree conservation status
- Neighbouring redevelopments (may overshadow or create boundary disputes)
- Subsidence or settlement evidence (cracks in walls, doors not closing)
