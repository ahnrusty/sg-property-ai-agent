# Selling Process

For selling residential property (HDB resale or private).

## Pre-sale prep

1. **Resolve outstanding loan**: Know payoff figure, redemption notice period (typically 3 months for some packages or pay penalty).
2. **Compute CPF accrued interest**: principal + 2.5% p.a. compounded; must be returned to OA on sale.
3. **Compute net proceeds**: sale price − outstanding loan − CPF refund − agent commission − legal fees − any SSD.
4. **Engage lawyer**: For conveyancing.
5. **Decide pricing**: Compare last 6 to 12 months of transactions, average $/psf, current listings, time of year.
6. **Stage the unit**: De-clutter, deep clean, minor cosmetic touch-ups (paint, light fittings).
7. **Photography**: Professional photos significantly increase listing engagement.
8. **Listing strategy**: Exclusive agent vs open listing.

## Pricing strategy

| Strategy | When |
|----------|------|
| Above market (testing) | Strong unique features, no urgency, willing to wait 3 to 6 months |
| At market | Standard sale, comparable to recent transactions |
| Below market | Need to move fast, distress sale, market softening |

Be aware: an over-priced listing that sits for 3+ months becomes stale. Re-listing at lower price signals desperation.

## Agent commission

Standard market practice for **seller** to pay both sides:

- HDB: 2% of sale price by seller; buyer's agent often gets co-broke from seller's agent.
- Private resale: 2% by seller (sometimes 1.5% for high-value); buyer's agent gets co-broke.

Commission is negotiable. Some sellers offer 2.5% to incentivise faster sale.

Inclusive of GST or not? Confirm in writing.

## SSD check

Seller's Stamp Duty applies if you sell residential within 3 years of purchase:

| Holding period | SSD rate |
|----------------|----------|
| ≤ 1 year | 12% |
| > 1 to ≤ 2 years | 8% |
| > 2 to ≤ 3 years | 4% |
| > 3 years | 0% |

Computed on the higher of sale price or market value. If you bought within 3 years, factor this in before deciding to sell.

## Marketing channels

- PropertyGuru, 99.co, EdgeProp listings (paid placements rank higher)
- Social media (your agent or you)
- Open house events
- Direct outreach to your agent's buyer database
- Co-broking with other agencies

## Viewings

- Vacate during viewings or stand back; let agent run it
- Have all relevant documents ready: maintenance fee receipts, MCST notices, floorplans, defects records
- Be ready to answer: why selling, when key handover, what's included, recent renovations, any noise issues
- Be honest about defects you know of; deliberate concealment can void OTP

## Offers

Offers typically come in via your agent. Each offer should specify:

- Buyer name and residency
- Offer price
- Completion timeline
- Deposit amount
- Conditions (subject to loan approval, valuation)
- Validity period

Counter-offer in writing. Don't accept verbal offers; require written.

## OTP issuance (seller perspective)

When you accept an offer:

1. Buyer's agent (or your agent) prepares OTP.
2. Buyer pays option fee (1% for private, agreed for HDB).
3. You as seller sign OTP and acknowledge receipt of option fee.
4. Buyer has option period to exercise (14 days private, 21 days HDB).
5. If buyer exercises, transaction proceeds to completion.
6. If buyer doesn't exercise, you keep option fee and re-list.

## Completion (seller side)

- Lawyer handles title transfer at SLA
- Buyer's bank releases loan to your lawyer's client account
- Your lawyer disburses: pays off your outstanding mortgage, refunds CPF (principal + accrued interest), deducts legal fees and SSD if any, transfers net proceeds to you
- Hand over keys, access cards, mailbox keys, warranties, owner's manuals
- Final meter readings
- Update IRAS, utilities, town council / MCST of change of ownership

## Bridging loan (if buying next home)

If you're selling and buying simultaneously:

- **Bridging loan**: Short-term loan from bank, typically 6 months, secured against existing property. Bridges cash gap between buying new and selling existing.
- **Decoupling**: Separate strategy if married; transfer existing property fully to one spouse, freeing the other to buy with no ABSD.
- **15-month wait-out**: If selling private and buying HDB resale, you trigger this rule.
- **6-month ABSD remission window**: If buying second property and selling first, must dispose within 6 months of OTP (resale) or NVP (new launch).

Plan the transaction sequence with your agent, lawyer, and bank early.

## Tax on sale

- **No capital gains tax** in Singapore for residential property (key advantage vs other jurisdictions).
- SSD only if within 3-year holding period.
- Property tax pro-rated to date of completion.
- If rental income was reported, ensure no carry-forward issues.

## Net proceeds worked example

Sale price: $1,800,000
- Outstanding loan: $800,000
- CPF refund (principal + accrued interest): $350,000
- Agent commission (2% + GST): $39,240
- Legal fees: $2,800
- SSD: $0 (held > 3 years)

Net cash to seller: $1,800,000 − $800,000 − $350,000 − $39,240 − $2,800 = **$607,960**

The CPF portion ($350k) returns to your OA, where it continues to earn 2.5% interest.

## Common seller mistakes

- Over-pricing initially, then chasing market down with multiple cuts (each cut signals weakness)
- Cluttered, dirty viewings
- Refusing reasonable offers in hope of a better one that doesn't materialise
- Not preparing replacement housing before sale completes (forced rental at premium)
- Misjudging SSD if selling within 3 years
- Forgetting CPF accrued interest (smaller-than-expected cash out)
- Not co-broking (limits buyer reach)
