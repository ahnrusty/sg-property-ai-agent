# Upgrade and Downgrade Paths

Comprehensive playbook for every common residential transition in Singapore:
**HDB → Condo**, **Condo → Landed**, **Landed → Condo**, **Condo → HDB**, plus rarer paths (HDB → Landed, Landed → HDB).

Every transition has three levers: **sequencing** (sell first vs buy first vs hold both), **ABSD strategy** (avoid, remit, decouple, or absorb), and **financing** (cash, CPF, loan, bridging). Get these right and the move costs only what it should. Get them wrong and you lose six figures.

## The four sequencing strategies

| Strategy | Mechanism | Best for | Risks |
|----------|-----------|----------|-------|
| **Sell First** | Dispose current → live in interim rental → buy next | ABSD-free path; tight liquidity | 2-3 months bridging rental, double move, market timing |
| **Buy First, Sell Later** | Buy next → pay ABSD upfront → sell current within 6 months → apply for ABSD refund | SC married couples upgrading from HDB or single private; continuity of housing | 6-month deadline is hard; missed = ABSD forfeited; LTV drops to 45% while holding both |
| **Decoupling (Private only)** | Transfer joint share to one spouse → other spouse buys new at first-property ABSD | Couples keeping current property for rental income; high ABSD savings | BSD on transfer (~$20-50k); CPF refund; loan must qualify in sole name; IRAS scrutiny |
| **Stagger / Bridge** | Use bridging loan to overlap purchase and sale | Cashflow gap < 6 months; strong income | Bridging interest 4-6% pa; tight execution |

The "right" path depends on: your residency status, marital status, age (for senior exemptions), liquidity, urgency, and whether you want to retain the current property.

## Path 1: HDB → Condo (the upgrader classic)

The most common transition. Roughly 60-70% of new private buyers in OCR/RCR are HDB upgraders.

### Eligibility prerequisites

- HDB **MOP fulfilled** (5 years for Standard, 10 years for Plus/Prime)
- For the new condo: standard private property rules (no MOP, no income ceiling)
- ABSD trigger: condo is your 2nd residential property if you still own HDB at OTP

### The three viable plays

#### Play A: Sell HDB First (zero ABSD)

```
T-0: List HDB
T+8 to T+12 weeks: HDB OTP signed
T+16 to T+20 weeks: HDB completion → cash + CPF refund + rental temporary
T+20: Sign OTP on condo as first property (0% ABSD)
T+28 to T+32: Move into condo
```

**Pros**:
- **$0 ABSD** (saves $200k–$500k on typical $1-2.5M condo)
- Maximum LTV 75% on new condo
- Cash + CPF position known before committing

**Cons**:
- 2-3 months bridging rental ($3-6k/mo)
- Double move (HDB → rental → condo)
- Market risk: condo prices could rise during your wait
- HDB sale price uncertain at planning stage

#### Play B: Buy Condo First, Sell HDB Within 6 Months (ABSD refund)

```
T-0: Sign OTP on condo (pay 20% ABSD = ~$300-500k upfront in CASH)
T+0: Start marketing HDB aggressively
T+8 to T+16 weeks: HDB OTP signed and exercised
T+16 to T+24 weeks: HDB completion (must be within 6 months of condo legal completion)
T+24+: Apply to IRAS for ABSD refund
T+30: Refund typically received within 6 weeks
```

**Pros**:
- No interim rental
- Single move
- Condo locked in at known price

**Cons**:
- **6-month deadline is HARD**. Miss it by one day and the ABSD is permanently forfeited. IRAS does not grant case-by-case extensions.
- LTV drops to **45%** while holding both properties (55% down payment)
- ABSD cash float of $200-500k for up to 6 months
- HDB market softness = real risk of missing deadline
- Bank may require bridging loan if cashflow tight (4-6% pa interest)

**Eligibility**: Must be Singapore Citizen **married couple** buying jointly. SC singles, SPR couples, and SC+Foreigner couples do NOT qualify for the remission. Citizens of FTA partner countries (Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway, Switzerland, USA) may qualify for SC ABSD treatment under their respective FTAs.

#### Play C: Stretch with Bridging Loan

For SC singles, SPR couples, or other ineligible-for-remission cases who still want continuity:

- Sell HDB but timing misaligned with condo purchase
- Bank bridging loan against HDB equity (6 months typical, up to 12)
- Interest 4-6% pa, only on drawn amount
- Use bridging proceeds for condo down payment; repay on HDB sale

**Not a real ABSD strategy** — bridging just smooths cashflow; ABSD still applies if you genuinely hold two properties at OTP.

### Decoupling — usually NOT available for HDB

HDB flats cannot be decoupled between spouses except under specific circumstances (e.g., divorce court order, mutual agreement under HDB-approved schemes for specific transfer cases). For HDB upgraders, decoupling is generally not a path. Plan around sell-first or buy-first remission.

### Cashflow snapshot ($720k HDB → $1.85M condo, SC couple)

| Item | Sell First | Buy First (with remission) |
|------|------------|-----------------------------|
| HDB sale proceeds (cash) | $300k | $300k (within 6 months) |
| CPF refund | $220k | $220k |
| Condo down payment 25% | $462k | $1.02M (55% on $1.85M while holding 2) |
| BSD | $54,600 | $54,600 |
| ABSD upfront | $0 | $370,000 (refunded later) |
| Legal fees (HDB + condo) | $5,500 | $5,500 |
| Bridging rental (3 months) | $12,000 | $0 |
| **Total cash needed upfront** | **~$160k** | **~$620k** (ABSD float) |
| **Total cash after all transactions** | $300k - condo cash portion | $300k - condo cash portion (ABSD refunded) |

Sell First wins on cash efficiency. Buy First wins on convenience.

## Path 2: Condo → Landed (the wealth-building upgrade)

Less common but high-value. Landed is the apex of Singapore residential.

### Eligibility prerequisites

- **Foreigners cannot buy mainland landed without LDAU approval** (Land Dealings Approval Unit, SLA). Approval is discretionary, requires demonstrated economic contribution, takes weeks to months, and is rarely granted.
- **Singapore PRs** also cannot buy landed without LDAU approval. Same constraints.
- Only **Singapore Citizens** have unrestricted access to mainland landed. (Sentosa Cove is the sole exception where foreigners can buy one landed for own-occupation.)
- Standard ABSD and TDSR/MSR rules apply.

### Common scenarios

| Scenario | Strategy |
|----------|----------|
| SC couple, sell condo to buy landed | Same playbook as HDB→Condo: Sell First OR Buy First with 6-month remission |
| SC couple, keep condo for rental + buy landed | **Decoupling** is the high-leverage move (see below) |
| SC family, multiple properties, buy landed as primary | ABSD on 2nd or 3rd property is significant; decoupling or trust structures considered (with tax counsel) |

### The decoupling play (powerful for condo → landed)

If both spouses are joint owners of the condo (typical), they can:

1. **Transfer entire share** from Spouse A to Spouse B
2. Spouse B becomes sole owner of the condo
3. Spouse A now owns **zero properties** for ABSD purposes
4. Spouse A buys landed as **first property** at 0% ABSD
5. Both properties retained; one in each name

**Cost of decoupling** (for a $2M condo, transferring 50% = $1M):
- BSD on transferred share ($1M): $24,600
- Legal fees (2 separate lawyers): $4-6k
- Mortgage refinancing in remaining spouse's sole name: $1-3k
- **Total**: ~$30-35k
- CPF refund from Spouse A to their OA: principal + accrued interest (cashflow not cost)

**Savings vs paying ABSD on a $3.5M landed**:
- ABSD on 2nd property (SC) at $3.5M = 20% = **$700,000**
- Decoupling cost: ~$35,000
- **Net savings: $665,000**

### Decoupling requirements (don't skip)

- **Genuine transaction**: market-value pricing, supported by professional valuation
- **Separate legal counsel** for each spouse (joint counsel is an IRAS red flag)
- **Timing**: complete decoupling fully before the second property's OTP (back-to-back transactions invite scrutiny)
- **Mortgage qualification**: remaining spouse must independently qualify for the refinanced loan in sole name (TDSR test)
- **CPF flows**: transferring spouse's CPF refunded to their OA in full (principal + 2.5% accrued)
- **IRAS may challenge** if the arrangement is clearly contrived purely to evade ABSD

### Landed-specific upgrade considerations

- **Plot value vs house value**: in 99-year leasehold landed, lease decay matters. For freehold landed, the play is land bank.
- **Rebuild ROI**: many buyers acquire older landed with intent to rebuild. Construction cost $350-1,500/psf GFA depending on spec. Add 12-24 months timeline.
- **Conservation**: parts of Joo Chiat, Blair Plain, Cairnhill have conservation status restricting demolition.
- **Plot ratio**: URA Master Plan governs GFA potential. Older properties often under-built.
- **Maintenance**: landed has higher ongoing costs (lawn, security, plumbing, roof) vs condo's pooled maintenance.

### Cashflow snapshot ($1.85M condo → $4M freehold landed, SC couple, decoupling)

| Item | Without decoupling (Sell First) | With decoupling |
|------|--------------------------------|------------------|
| Sell condo: net cash | $900k | n/a (kept) |
| Sell condo: CPF refund | $280k | n/a |
| Buy landed: down payment 25% | $1.0M | $1.0M (Spouse A first property: 75% LTV) |
| BSD on landed | $179,600 | $179,600 |
| ABSD | $0 | $0 (decoupled spouse buys as first) |
| Decoupling cost (BSD + legal) | n/a | $35,000 |
| Bridging rental (3 months) | $18,000 | $0 |
| **Hard cash needed** | **~$700k** (cash + CPF mix) | **~$700k + decoupling cost** |
| **Rental income retained** | $0 | ~$5-6k/month from condo |
| **Net portfolio** | 1 landed | 1 landed + 1 condo |

## Path 3: Landed → Condo (the downsize)

Common for retirees, empty-nesters, or those wanting less maintenance.

### Why people downsize from landed

- Adult children moved out; landed space no longer needed
- Retirement income optimisation (sale proceeds fund retirement; condo facilities reduce upkeep)
- Health and mobility (single-level living, condo gym/pool, security)
- Capital release: convert illiquid landed equity into cash and CPF top-ups

### No special restrictions

Landed sellers do not face seller's restrictions specific to landed (no MOP equivalent). SSD applies only if held < 3 years (uncommon for landed).

For the condo purchase:
- Standard private property rules
- ABSD: depends on residency, marital status, and property count after purchase

### Sequencing options

| Strategy | When |
|----------|------|
| Sell First | Simplest; clean cash position before condo OTP |
| Buy First (6-month remission) | If SC married couple, buying as second property → sell landed within 6 months to refund ABSD |
| Keep Landed, Buy Condo | Decoupling possible if landed is jointly owned; ABSD on condo otherwise |

### Retiree advantages (age 55+)

If you're 55 or above:

1. **CPF refund** from landed sale tops up your **Retirement Account (RA)** to the Full Retirement Sum (FRS) first, before any excess goes to OA.
2. **FRS for 2026**: $220,400 (rises annually). FRS for 2027: $228,200.
3. Higher RA balance = higher monthly CPF LIFE payouts for life.
4. This converts illiquid landed equity into both lump-sum cash AND lifetime retirement income.

**Worked example**: Sell $5M landed, fully paid off (no outstanding mortgage), CPF used + accrued interest $500k. You're 60 years old.

- Sale proceeds: $5,000,000
- Less agent commission (2% + GST): $109,000
- Less legal fees: $3,500
- CPF refund to RA (tops up to FRS $220,400, balance to OA): $500,000
- **Net cash to you**: ~$4,387,500
- **RA boost**: significant; CPF LIFE payouts increase materially

Buy a $2.2M condo from the cash:
- Down payment 25%: $550k
- BSD: $74,600
- ABSD: $0 (no other properties)
- Legal: $3,500
- Remaining liquid cash: $3,759,400 for investment, lifestyle, or family

The downsize unlocks **~$3.7M of liquid retirement capital** while you still live well in a condo.

### Cashflow snapshot ($5M freehold landed → $2.2M condo, SC couple, both 60)

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Landed sale | $5,000,000 |
| Less mortgage (assume paid off) | $0 |
| Less CPF refund (to RA + OA) | -$500,000 |
| Less agent commission | -$109,000 |
| Less legal fees | -$3,500 |
| **Net cash** | **$4,387,500** |
| Condo purchase | $2,200,000 |
| Down payment (cash, no need for high LTV at retirement) | $2,200,000 (all cash, optional) |
| BSD | $74,600 |
| Legal | $3,500 |
| **Cash after condo** | **$2,109,400** |

Massive retirement liquidity release. Plus boosted CPF LIFE.

## Path 4: Condo → HDB (the contentious downgrade)

The most regulation-heavy transition. Singapore policy actively discourages this to prevent private capital pushing up HDB resale prices.

### The 15-month wait-out rule (introduced Sep 2022)

If you sell your private property and want to buy a **non-subsidised HDB resale flat**, you must wait **15 months after the disposal completion date** before you are eligible to apply.

This applies to:
- Condominium owners
- Landed property owners
- Owners of overseas residential property
- Current and previous private property owners

### The senior exemption

Singapore Citizens aged **55 and above** are exempt from the 15-month wait-out period if:
- Both spouses are 55 or older (if buying jointly)
- Buying a **4-room or smaller** resale flat, OR
- A 2-room Flexi flat, OR
- A Community Care Apartment (CCA) intended for seniors

Seniors transitioning to retirement housing can move directly without the wait.

### Appeals process

HDB does grant case-by-case waivers. Between Sep 2022 and Mar 2025, HDB received ~5,500 appeals. Approximately **25%** were approved.

Successful appeals usually involve:
- Documentary proof of OTP/sale commitment **before Sep 30, 2022** (the policy date)
- Financial difficulties with no alternative housing
- Extenuating family circumstances (medical, elder care)

If your situation has genuine merit, file the appeal. But don't bank on approval.

### Brand-new BTO from private background

If you want a brand-new BTO (not resale), the wait-out is even longer: **30 months** after private property disposal, plus you'd need to ballot like everyone else.

### What to do during the 15 months

| Option | Considerations |
|--------|----------------|
| Rent | $2-5k/mo typical for HDB-equivalent space; budget for it |
| Stay with family | If feasible; saves cash |
| Renew condo lease (if you haven't sold) | Defer sale until you're closer to HDB eligibility; check market timing |
| Buy private second property (smaller) | Bridge with rental income from your old place |

### Income and grant considerations

For HDB resale after wait-out:
- Income ceiling for HDB loan eligibility: $14k (family)
- Income ceiling for grants (Family Grant, EHG, PHG): same
- If your income is above ceiling, you can still buy HDB resale but only with **bank loan** (HDB loan unavailable) and **no grants**

### Cashflow snapshot ($1.85M condo → $650k HDB 4-room, SC couple aged 50)

```
Year 0: Sell condo
- Sale proceeds: $1,850,000
- Less mortgage (assume $400k outstanding): -$400,000
- Less CPF refund: -$280,000
- Less agent commission (2% + GST): -$40,330
- Less legal fees: -$2,800
- Net cash: $1,126,870
- CPF returned to OA: $280,000

Year 0 to Year 1.25: WAIT 15 months
- Interim rental: 15 months × $3,500/mo = $52,500
- Net cash after rental: $1,074,370

Year 1.25: Buy HDB 4-room resale at $650k
- 5% cash deposit: $32,500
- 20% down payment: $130,000 (CPF + cash)
- BSD: $14,400
- Legal: $2,800
- Bank loan: $487,500 at 2.5% over 25 years ≈ $2,189/mo
- Cash position: $1,074k - $32.5k - $97.5k - $14.4k - $2.8k = ~$927k liquid

Result: Comfortable retirement reserves, lower mortgage, lifestyle right-sized.
```

If both spouses were 55+ and buying a 4-room or smaller, they could skip the 15-month wait entirely and save $52k+ in rental.

## Rarer paths

### HDB → Landed (skip the condo step)

- Eligibility: SC only for mainland landed
- ABSD: still 0% if you sell HDB before OTP on landed (or use 6-month remission)
- Loan: landed prices typically $3-10M+; bank IPA scrutiny higher
- TDSR / LTV apply same as private
- Cash component: 5% min cash on first property

The play is identical to HDB → Condo from sequencing perspective. The challenge is purely affordability.

### Landed → HDB

- Subject to the **15-month wait-out** (or senior exemption)
- All landed sale CPF refund rules apply (RA top-up at 55+)
- This is essentially a retirement downsize; treat as a Landed → Condo with downsize destination = HDB

### Condo → Condo (sideways)

- Common for changing area, school catchment, or unit size
- ABSD: if buying as second, 20% (SC), 30% (SPR), 60% (FR), with 6-month remission available for SC married couples
- Decoupling viable if keeping the original for rental
- LTV: 75% on first sale-then-buy, 45% on holding both

### HDB → EC

- Treated like HDB → Condo for eligibility, but EC has its own MSR/MOP rules
- EC: 5-year MOP, $16k income ceiling at purchase
- ABSD: same logic as condo
- Resale only to SC/SPR for first 10 years

## Decision matrix

Pick your path:

| Your situation | Recommended strategy |
|----------------|----------------------|
| HDB upgrader, SC couple, comfortable liquidity | Buy First with 6-month remission |
| HDB upgrader, SC couple, tight cash | Sell First |
| HDB upgrader, single SC | Sell First (no remission available) |
| HDB upgrader, SC+SPR | Sell First (remission depends on SPR spouse disposing too) |
| Condo upgrader, want to keep condo for rental | Decoupling |
| Condo upgrader to landed, SC couple, sell condo | Sell First or Buy First with remission |
| Landed downsize at 55+ | Direct sell + buy; use CPF refund to boost RA |
| Condo to HDB, under 55 | Plan for 15-month rental; consider appeal if merit |
| Condo to HDB, both 55+, 4-room or smaller | Direct downsize; no wait-out |
| Condo to HDB, doesn't fit senior exemption | Wait 15 months or appeal |

## Common mistakes (lose six figures)

1. **Missing the 6-month ABSD remission deadline** by underestimating HDB sale timeline. Always plan for 4-month execution; leave 2 months buffer.
2. **Underestimating LTV drop** while holding two properties (45% not 75%). Cashflow shortage at OTP.
3. **Decoupling without separate counsel** — IRAS may challenge as contrived.
4. **Decoupling when remaining spouse can't qualify for sole-name loan** — refinancing fails.
5. **Ignoring the 15-month wait-out** when planning condo → HDB. Discovering it after exercising OTP.
6. **Senior exemption miscount**: only one spouse 55, other 54 → doesn't qualify yet. Wait or rethink.
7. **CPF refund miscalculation**: assuming proceeds are mostly cash, then discovering large CPF return. Plan with CPF Home Calculator.
8. **SSD trigger on accidental short hold**: condo bought 30 months ago, decision to upgrade now. Selling before month 36 = 4% SSD. Wait the extra 6 months.
9. **Bridging loan cost underestimation**: 4-6% pa, plus admin fees, plus tight deadlines.
10. **Not pre-qualifying loan in sole name** before decoupling — surprise rejection mid-transaction.

- [ ] HDB MOP status (Resale Portal)
