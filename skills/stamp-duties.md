# Stamp Duties: BSD, ABSD, SSD

All rates valid as of late 2025 / 2026. Always re-verify on IRAS before signing.

## Buyer's Stamp Duty (BSD)

Payable by every buyer of immovable property. Computed on the higher of purchase price or market value.

### Residential rates (from 14 Feb 2023)

| Consideration band | Rate |
|--------------------|------|
| First $180,000 | 1% |
| Next $180,000 (up to $360,000) | 2% |
| Next $640,000 (up to $1,000,000) | 3% |
| Next $500,000 (up to $1,500,000) | 4% |
| Next $1,500,000 (up to $3,000,000) | 5% |
| Above $3,000,000 | 6% |

### Non-residential rates

| Band | Rate |
|------|------|
| First $180,000 | 1% |
| Next $180,000 | 2% |
| Next $640,000 | 3% |
| Next $500,000 | 4% |
| Above $1,500,000 | 5% |

### Quick formulas (residential)

- $1M: $24,600
- $1.5M: $44,600
- $2M: $69,600
- $2.5M: $94,600
- $3M: $119,600
- $4M: $179,600

## Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty (ABSD)

From 27 April 2023. On top of BSD.

| Buyer profile | 1st residential | 2nd residential | 3rd and beyond |
|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| Singapore Citizen | 0% | 20% | 30% |
| Singapore PR | 5% | 30% | 35% |
| Foreigner | 60% | 60% | 60% |
| Entity (company, trust) | 65% | 65% | 65% |
| Housing developer | 5% non-remittable + 35% remittable | same | same |

### Profile timing

ABSD profile is fixed at the date OTP is granted, not at completion. If your residency changes between OTP and completion, the earlier date governs.

### Joint purchase rule

When buying jointly, the **highest** applicable ABSD rate among all buyers applies. Example: SC + Foreigner spouse buying first property together = 60% (foreigner rate).

### Counting properties

- Includes residential property held anywhere in the world that you have beneficial interest in.
- Includes property held via a trust where you are the beneficiary.
- Does not include commercial / industrial.

## ABSD remissions

### Married couple (SC + SC, SC + SPR, or SPR + SPR) buying matrimonial home

If at least one spouse is an SC and you sell your existing residential property within 6 months of:
- the date of OTP for resale, or
- the issue of Notice of Vacant Possession for new launch, whichever applies,

you can apply for ABSD refund on the second property purchase. Both names must be on both transactions.

### Foreigner spouse of SC under Free Trade Agreements

Citizens of Iceland, Liechtenstein, Norway, Switzerland, and the United States are accorded the same ABSD treatment as Singapore Citizens for residential purchases, under the relevant FTAs. Verify current list on IRAS.

### Developer ABSD (5-year sales rule)

Developers pay 5% non-remittable ABSD upfront on the land. The remaining 35% can be remitted if all units in the project are sold within 5 years of the option to purchase the land.

## Seller's Stamp Duty (SSD)

Payable by sellers who dispose of residential property within the holding period.

### Residential SSD (since 11 March 2017)

| Holding period | Rate |
|----------------|------|
| ≤ 1 year | 12% |
| > 1 to ≤ 2 years | 8% |
| > 2 to ≤ 3 years | 4% |
| > 3 years | 0% |

Computed on the higher of selling price or market value.

### Industrial SSD

Separate schedule, longer holding periods, different rates. Verify on IRAS.

## Payment timing

- BSD and ABSD: within **14 days** of OTP signing if signed in Singapore, **30 days** if signed overseas.
- SSD: within 14 days of disposal contract signing.

Late payment penalty: up to 4 times the unpaid amount under the Stamp Duties Act.

## Payment via CPF OA

- BSD: yes, CPF OA can be used.
- ABSD on a new launch property: yes, CPF OA can be used (cash reimbursement model).
- ABSD on resale: no, must be paid in cash.
- SSD: paid from sale proceeds, not CPF.
