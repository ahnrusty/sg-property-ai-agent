# New Launch (Buying from Developer)

For brand-new private condos and apartments sold directly by the developer.

## Process

1. **Preview / VVIP**: Closed-door preview for selected agents and buyers, usually 1 to 2 weeks before public launch.
2. **Sales gallery / showflat**: Public viewing. Marketing brochures, scale models, sample units.
3. **Booking**: Pay 5% booking fee in cash. Receive Option to Purchase (OTP). 14-day option period.
4. **Exercise OTP**: Sign Sale & Purchase Agreement (S&P), pay 15% (10% by cash or CPF + 5% earlier booking).
5. **Stamp duties**: BSD and ABSD payable within 14 days of OTP signing.
6. **Construction**: 2 to 4 years typical.
7. **Notice of Vacant Possession (NVP)**: Developer notifies you when ready for handover. Usually means TOP achieved.
8. **CSC**: Certificate of Statutory Completion, ~1 year after TOP.
9. **DLP**: Defects Liability Period, typically 12 months from NVP, during which developer must rectify defects.

## Progressive Payment Scheme (PPS)

Payments are tied to construction milestones:

| Milestone | % of price |
|-----------|------------|
| Booking | 5% (cash) |
| Sign S&P (within ~8 weeks) | 15% (10% top-up) |
| Foundation completed | 10% |
| Reinforced concrete framework completed | 10% |
| Brick walls completed | 5% |
| Roofing / ceiling completed | 5% |
| Door sub-frames and electrical wiring completed | 5% |
| Car park, drains, road completed | 5% |
| TOP (Notice of Vacant Possession) | 25% |
| CSC | 15% |

Loan drawdown follows the same schedule, so monthly mortgage builds up over the construction period.

## Deferred Payment Scheme (DPS)

For some completed projects sold by developers, DPS may be offered: pay 20% upfront, balance 80% on completion. Discount typically not given; convenience for buyers who want to avoid progressive cashflow. Watch for premium pricing.

## Showflat reality check

Showflats are designed to flatter. Verify:

- **Ceiling height**: showflat may have higher ceilings than actual unit
- **Wall thickness**: showflat walls may be thinner for spaciousness illusion
- **Furniture scale**: bespoke smaller furniture makes rooms look bigger
- **Finishes**: showflat finishes may be upgraded vs standard package
- **Balcony**: actual depth and railing may differ
- **Bathroom**: vanity, mirror, taps may be different brand
- **Yard / utility**: many showflats omit the service yard

Ask for the **as-built specifications schedule** in the brochure and the **scale floor plan with actual dimensions**.

## Strata, internal, and balcony area

| Term | What it means |
|------|----------------|
| Strata area | Total area including walls, balcony, AC ledge, yard, planters |
| Internal area | Actual liveable interior (excludes balcony, AC ledge) |
| Balcony area | Often 5 to 10% of strata area |
| Aircon ledge | 1 to 3% of strata area |

You pay $/psf on strata. Compare projects on internal area per dollar for true value. Some developments have very large unusable spaces inflating strata.

## Defects Liability Period

- 12 months from NVP for buildings (PPVC / standard construction)
- 6 months for some non-structural elements
- Walk through the unit with a defects checklist on handover
- Engage a defects inspection service ($300 to $800) for thorough review
- Submit defects list to developer within 7 to 14 days
- Re-inspect at end of DLP

Common defects: tile lippage, paint touch-ups, hairline cracks, door alignment, leaks, marble stains, electrical fittings, aircon condensation.

## Maintenance fee first AGM

The developer-set maintenance fee is usually lower than what is sustainable. After the first AGM (~1 to 2 years post-TOP), expect a 10 to 25% increase as the MCST takes over and right-sizes the budget.

## ABSD remission window

If buying as second property and intending to sell first:

- For new launch: must dispose of first property within **6 months of NVP** to qualify for remission (married couple rule).
- This is much longer than the 6-month-from-OTP window for resale purchases, because construction takes years.
- Plan first-property sale timing carefully.

## Common new launch pitfalls

- Buying preview prices that turn out to be similar to launch prices (less "early bird" than promoted)
- Underestimating construction delays (12-month delays are common)
- Not factoring in 5 to 10% additional cost (legal, stamp duties, defects rectification, fittings, curtains, electricals)
- Buying in a project that ends up over-supplied at TOP (price stagnates for 2 to 3 years)
- Discovering noise from a confirmed but unbuilt MRT line or new development next door
- ABSD remission timing miscalculated, losing 6-figure refund

## Worked cashflow example

$2M private condo, first property, SC buyer, 30-year tenure, indicative rate 2%:

| Stage | Amount | Source |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Booking fee (5%) | $100,000 | Cash |
| OTP exercise top-up (15% − 5%) | $200,000 | CPF + Cash |
| BSD | $69,600 | CPF OK |
| ABSD | $0 | First property |
| Legal fees | $3,500 | Cash |
| Stamp duty admin | $500 | Cash |
| **Year 0 outlay** | **$373,600** | |
| Monthly mortgage at TOP (75% LTV, full $1.5M loan) | ~$5,545 | CPF + Cash |
| Maintenance fee | ~$400 | Cash |
| Property tax (annual, owner-occupied AV ~$36k) | ~$1,400/yr | Cash |

During construction, partial drawdown means monthly mortgage builds up gradually rather than starting at $5,545.
