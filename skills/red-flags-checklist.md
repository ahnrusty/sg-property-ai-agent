# Red Flags Checklist

Patterns that should make you pause, ask harder questions, or walk away.

## Listing red flags

- [ ] Photos are clearly from years ago (furniture style, calendar visible, dated finishes)
- [ ] "Recently renovated" but no before/after photos or contractor invoice
- [ ] $/psf significantly below comparable transactions (often hides a defect or lease issue)
- [ ] $/psf significantly above comparable transactions without unique justification
- [ ] Vague descriptions: "near MRT" (verify km), "spacious" (check sqft)
- [ ] Listing repeatedly relisted / re-posted (check listing dates across portals)
- [ ] Multiple owners selling in the same building simultaneously
- [ ] Same unit listed by multiple agents at different prices
- [ ] Agent CEA number missing or not findable on CEA register
- [ ] Listing claims "freehold" but address is in a known leasehold-only estate (verify SLA)

## Agent red flags

- [ ] Pressure to sign OTP same day or "before someone else gets it"
- [ ] Refuses to provide MCST minutes for condo
- [ ] Refuses to schedule second viewing
- [ ] Refuses to disclose seller motivation
- [ ] Quotes property tax / maintenance fees but won't provide invoices
- [ ] Pushes specific lawyer or bank exclusively
- [ ] Says CEA number is "with my BCA" (suspect; verify directly)
- [ ] Speaks for the seller but doesn't have written authority (sometimes agent does not have full mandate)
- [ ] Asks for cash option fee without proper receipt

## Unit red flags

- [ ] Fresh paint on ceilings (often hides water staining)
- [ ] Strong perfume / air freshener (masking smells)
- [ ] Furniture deliberately placed to cover walls or floors
- [ ] Dehumidifier running (humidity issue)
- [ ] No power for aircon test (cannot verify aircon condition)
- [ ] Locked rooms ("under renovation", "tenant's stuff")
- [ ] Recent painting that doesn't match the rest of the wall
- [ ] Mould around windows, behind furniture, in bathrooms
- [ ] Tile lippage on floors (poor reno work)
- [ ] Cracks running diagonally across walls (structural, not just hairline)
- [ ] Sloping floors (test with a ball or marble)
- [ ] Door / window alignment off (could indicate building movement)

## Building red flags (condo)

- [ ] Lift performance poor (long waits, frequent breakdowns)
- [ ] Common areas dirty, broken fixtures unrepaired
- [ ] Empty pool or pool with cloudy water
- [ ] Lobby, mailboxes, signage in poor state
- [ ] High vacancy (many "For Rent" / "For Sale" signs on doors)
- [ ] Visible water damage in stairwells or carpark
- [ ] Cracks in carpark slabs or walls
- [ ] Security guards bored, distracted, or absent

## Contract red flags

- [ ] OTP terms differ from what was verbally agreed
- [ ] Unusual completion timeline (very short or very long without reason)
- [ ] Indemnity clauses where buyer waives standard protections
- [ ] "As-is, where-is" sale without inspection allowance
- [ ] Seller exclusion of warranties for known issues
- [ ] Vague "all fixtures and fittings" without inventory list
- [ ] Non-standard deposit amount or deposit going to non-stakeholder account
- [ ] Clause allowing seller to keep deposit beyond standard option-fee forfeit
- [ ] Penalty clauses against buyer for delay caused by bank or stamp-duty processing

## Financial red flags

- [ ] Seller asking for cash payments outside official channels (potentially tax evasion or money laundering)
- [ ] CPF refund computation seller refuses to share (could indicate negative sale risk)
- [ ] Outstanding maintenance fees / property tax unpaid (becomes buyer's problem)
- [ ] Existing tenant on lease that the seller didn't disclose (buyer inherits tenancy)
- [ ] Mortgage redemption notice longer than completion timeline (closing may slip)
- [ ] Caveats or charges on title not disclosed by seller

## HDB-specific red flags

- [ ] MOP not yet satisfied (sale cannot complete)
- [ ] Seller previously rented out whole flat without HDB approval (penalty risk)
- [ ] Ethnic Integration Policy or SPR quota breach (cannot buy)
- [ ] Upgrading levy applicable but not disclosed
- [ ] Renovation done without HDB permit (rectification cost)
- [ ] Outstanding HDB conservancy charges
- [ ] Lease so short that bank loan tenure or CPF usage is heavily constrained

## Market-context red flags

- [ ] Seller pricing on hope of en-bloc that has no concrete timeline
- [ ] Seller benchmarking to peak market (e.g. 2022 prices) in a different cycle
- [ ] Heavy supply incoming nearby (new launches that will undercut your $/psf)
- [ ] URA Master Plan signals area is being de-prioritised (industrial conversion, expressway widening)
- [ ] Specific stack / unit type historically slow to resell

## Foreigner / cross-border red flags

- [ ] Foreigner buying landed without LDAU approval (illegal)
- [ ] Trust / company structure to evade ABSD (now 65%, no longer effective and legally risky)
- [ ] "Decoupling" advice that doesn't fit your tax residency or marital status
- [ ] Overseas spouse not on title but funding the deal (regulatory disclosure issues)

## When to walk away

If three or more red flags appear during due diligence, seriously reconsider. If any single red flag is legal (illegal sub-letting, undisclosed lease, unauthorised renovation, foreigner buying landed without approval), do not proceed regardless of price.

## When red flags can be negotiated

Many red flags are negotiable rather than dealbreakers. For example:

- Minor defects → price reduction or seller-funded rectification before completion
- Old maintenance issues with MCST → reduced price or seller paying first year of pending special levy
- Tenant in place → adjust completion timing or buy with tenant if rental terms work for you

The key: surface every red flag in writing, attach a dollar value, and counter accordingly.
