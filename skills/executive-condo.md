# Executive Condominium (EC)

ECs are a hybrid public-private housing model: built and sold by private developers but with HDB-like eligibility, MOP, and resale rules for the first 10 years. After 10 years they become fully private.

## Eligibility (buying from developer)

- At least one buyer must be a Singapore Citizen.
- All other co-applicants must be SC or SPR.
- Form a family nucleus (Public, Fiance/Fiancee, or Multi-Generation scheme).
- Average gross monthly household income ≤ **$16,000**.
- Not own any other local or overseas residential property in the **30 months** preceding application; if previously owned, must have disposed of it more than 30 months ago.
- Not have bought a subsidised flat from HDB or DBSS, or received CPF Housing Grant more than once.

## Resale eligibility

| Age of EC | Who can buy resale |
|-----------|---------------------|
| 0 to < 5 yrs (within MOP) | Cannot be resold |
| 5 to < 10 yrs (after MOP, before privatisation) | SC and SPR only; income ceiling no longer applies |
| ≥ 10 yrs (privatised) | Anyone including foreigners; subject to ABSD |

## Minimum Occupation Period

5 years from key collection. Same as standard HDB.

## CPF Housing Grant (EC)

Family Grant available for ECs purchased from developer, based on household income:

| Average gross monthly income | Grant amount |
|------------------------------|---------------|
| ≤ $10,000 | $30,000 |
| $10,001 to $11,000 | $20,000 |
| $11,001 to $12,000 | $10,000 |
| $12,001 to $16,000 | $0 |

Verify current grant on HDB website.

## Resale levy

If you previously took a housing subsidy (e.g. bought a BTO and selling now to buy an EC), a resale levy applies:

| Previous flat type | Resale levy (families) |
|--------------------|------------------------|
| 2-room | $15,000 |
| 3-room | $30,000 |
| 4-room | $40,000 |
| 5-room | $45,000 |
| Executive | $50,000 |
| EC | $55,000 |

Singles get half. Payable in cash at time of purchase.

## Loan rules

- MSR 30% applies (capped at 30% of gross income; same as HDB).
- TDSR 55% also applies.
- Bank loan only (no HDB loan for EC).
- LTV up to 75%, subject to 30-year max tenure and age-65 stress.

## Why ECs are popular

- Subsidised purchase price (typically $200 to $400 psf discount vs comparable private launches in the same area)
- Condo facilities (pool, gym, security, function room, BBQ pits)
- After 10 years, value typically converges toward private condo market price → potential capital gain
- Income ceiling at purchase is the only barrier; once you own, future income is unconstrained

## Risks

- MOP and resale restrictions limit your flexibility for 5 to 10 years
- If household income exceeds ceiling, you may not get the grant or the unit
- Subject to general property market risks
- Cannot rent out whole unit during MOP
- Often built in further-out OCR locations (acceptable trade-off for many)

## Common scenarios

**Young couple, household income $13,500, planning family:**
EC ticks many boxes. Within ceiling, condo lifestyle, 5-year MOP aligns with first-child timeline. Use HDB grant if income ≤ $12k average.

**Couple already owns HDB BTO, just finished MOP:**
Need to dispose HDB to apply for EC (cannot hold both as own-stay). Resale levy applies.

**SC + Foreigner spouse:**
Not eligible. Must be SC + SC/SPR. Look at private condo instead (with ABSD impact).

**Above $16k household income:**
Not eligible for EC purchase from developer. Buy EC resale after 5 years (no income ceiling) or go private.
