# Sell First vs Buy First — Sequencing Playbook

For anyone moving from one home to another, the single biggest financial decision is which transaction to execute first. This determines ABSD exposure, LTV, cash float, and exposure to market timing.

## The core trade-off

| | Sell First | Buy First |
|---|------------|-----------|
| ABSD | $0 (next property is your first/only at OTP) | Pay upfront, refund if eligible |
| LTV on new property | 75% (first property) | 45% (second property while holding both) |
| Cash needed upfront | Lower (just down payment) | Higher (down payment + ABSD + larger cash component) |
| Continuity of housing | Broken (need interim rental) | Maintained |
| Number of moves | 2 (current → rental → new) | 1 (current → new) |
| Market timing risk | High (sell low, buy high) | Lower (locked in) |
| Sale deadline pressure | None | 6 months (for ABSD remission) |
| Cashflow stress | Lower | Higher |
| Best for | Tight liquidity, single buyers, non-SC couples | SC couples with strong liquidity, no rental tolerance |

## The 6-month remission window in detail (ABSD)

Available to **Singapore Citizen married couples** buying jointly. Mechanism:

1. Buy second property; pay ABSD upfront at 20% (SC second) in cash
2. Within 6 months of the **second property's purchase date** (or **TOP/CSC for uncompleted property**), sell first property
3. Within 6 months of first property's sale completion, apply to IRAS for ABSD refund
4. Refund typically issued within 6 weeks of complete application

Specific deadlines:
- **Completed second property** (resale or already-built): 6 months from date of legal completion of the second property
- **Uncompleted second property** (new launch / Building Under Construction): 6 months from issuance of TOP or CSC, whichever is earlier

**No general extensions**. The only exception was a temporary COVID-era 6-month extension (now lapsed). Missing the deadline by **even one day** forfeits the ABSD permanently.

Application is **automatic** if IRAS systems detect the conditions are met. Manual application sometimes needed; coordinate with your conveyancing lawyer.

## Decision framework

Walk through these questions in order:

### Q1: Are you Singapore Citizens, married, both on both transactions?

- **No**: 6-month remission likely unavailable. **Sell First** is your default unless decoupling works.
- **Yes**: Q2.

### Q2: Can you carry the cash for ABSD + larger down payment for up to 6 months?

For a $1.8M condo:
- ABSD upfront: $360k
- Down payment 55% (since holding 2): $990k
- Total cash float: **$1.35M** while waiting for refund
- Plus BSD: $54.6k

If yes → Q3. If no → **Sell First** is the safer path.

### Q3: How confident are you in selling current property within 6 months?

- HDB resale typical sale time: 8-12 weeks once listed at right price
- Private condo resale: 12-26 weeks depending on segment
- Landed: 16-52+ weeks; harder to predict

If your current is HDB (and well-located, well-priced): high confidence; **Buy First** workable.
If condo or landed: pad timeline; consider **Sell First** unless market is hot.

### Q4: What is your market view?

If prices are rising: Buy First locks in the new property.
If prices are flat: indifferent.
If prices are falling: Sell First locks in your sale; you may catch the next purchase lower.

### Q5: Is interim rental tolerable?

For families with school-age kids: disruption is costly. Buy First wins on continuity.
For singles or empty-nesters: rental is fine. Sell First wins on cash efficiency.

## Sell First — execution checklist

1. List current property with experienced agent (exclusive 3-month)
2. Aggressive but defensible pricing (10-20% above target net = headroom for negotiation)
3. Stage and photograph well
4. Concurrent: bank IPA for next property (assume sold position)
5. Negotiate OTP within 30-90 days of listing
6. Complete sale: 8-12 weeks for HDB; 8-16 weeks for private
7. Move to interim rental (1-3 months recommended buffer)
8. Pull bank IPA → confirm
9. Issue OTP on next property as first property (0% ABSD)
10. Move into new home

**Interim rental cost**: $3,000-$6,000/month typical for HDB/condo equivalent. Budget $20-40k for the bridge.

## Buy First — execution checklist

1. Bank IPA for second property (LTV 45% on hold-both)
2. Cash + CPF model: ensure you have down payment + BSD + ABSD upfront
3. Engage lawyer
4. Issue OTP, exercise within 14 days for resale (different for new launch)
5. Pay BSD + ABSD within 14 days
6. **Day 0 of the 6-month clock**: legal completion date of second property (or TOP for new launch)
7. **Day 1**: list current property aggressively. Realistic pricing, not aspirational
8. **Day 30-60**: ideally OTP on current property
9. **Day 60-180**: complete current property sale
10. **Day 180** (latest): sale must be complete
11. **Day 180+**: apply for ABSD refund (or wait for automatic refund)
12. Refund credited typically within 6 weeks

If you slip past Day 180 by even a day: **ABSD forfeited**, no refund.

## Hybrid approaches

### Concurrent ("Same-day completion")

- Coordinate sale of current and purchase of next to complete on the **same day** or within days of each other
- Requires both seller and buyer (of your current property) cooperation, plus seller (of your next property) cooperation
- Lawyers manage the funds flow: sale proceeds → bank loan top-up + CPF → new purchase
- Often the cleanest if achievable but requires three-way alignment

### Conditional OTP

- Buy First with sale of current property as a **condition precedent** in the OTP for new property
- Seller of new property may not agree; reduces their leverage
- More common in soft markets; rare in hot markets

### Bridging loan

- Bank loan secured against your existing property's equity
- 6-month typical, up to 12 months
- 4-6% pa interest, only on drawn amount
- Useful for the gap between paying for new property and receiving sale proceeds of current
- **Not** a substitute for ABSD strategy

## Special cases

### Foreigner / SPR buying second

- 6-month remission **not available** (married couple SC-only rule)
- Decoupling possible if both spouses on title and one becomes property-free
- Otherwise pay ABSD: 60% (foreigner) or 30% (SPR second)
- Most realistic path: **Sell First**

### Single SC second property

- 6-month remission **not available** (married couple rule)
- Pay 20% ABSD or sell first
- For high-net-worth singles, may absorb 20% as investment overhead
- For mid-market, **Sell First** is the rational move

### Buying primarily for investment

- ABSD on second is part of the investment cost analysis
- Decoupling makes most sense here (keep original + buy new without ABSD)
- Sell First doesn't apply (you're keeping the original by definition)

### Multiple properties (3+)

- 30% (SC) or 35% (SPR) ABSD on 3rd+
- Trust structures or other arrangements increasingly common but increasingly scrutinised by IRAS
- Engage tax counsel before structuring

## Common sequencing mistakes

1. **Selling first without lining up next property** — market moves; you end up overpaying or in extended rental
2. **Buying first without bank IPA confirmed** — last-minute loan shortfall
3. **Buying first assuming HDB sells in 3 months** in a soft market
4. **Same-day completion attempts that fall apart** when one party slips
5. **Conditional OTP** rejected by seller in hot market; you lose preferred unit
6. **Bridging loan drawn but sale slips** — paying 5% interest on $1M for 6 months = $25k overhead
7. **Forgetting 6-month deadline is from TOP for new launch**, not from OTP
8. **Single SC trying to use remission** — not eligible
9. **Decoupling without IRAS-defensible documentation** — challenged later

## Verify before committing

- [ ] Bank IPA confirmed for the chosen sequencing path
- [ ] Cash + CPF positions modeled with realistic scenarios
- [ ] Lawyer engaged and briefed on the sequencing
- [ ] Real estate agent committed to aggressive marketing timeline
- [ ] ABSD remission eligibility verified (IRAS criteria)
- [ ] If buying first: ABSD cash float secured (savings or bridging line of credit)
- [ ] If selling first: interim housing arranged
- [ ] Stress-test for 6-month delay (Buy First) or 3-month delay (Sell First)
